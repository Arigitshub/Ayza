The Underlying Dynamics

The Transactional Analysis Drama Triangle

Rescuer (Ayza)

"Let me help you."

Victim (Children)

"Poor me, I can't cope."

Persecutor (The Situation)

"It's all your fault."

This model shows the predictable, dysfunctional roles Ayza and her children are trapped in. Ayza's 'Rescuer' role enables her children's 'Victim' stance, creating a cycle of dependency and resentment that harms everyone involved.

Attachment Style Imbalance

Ayza's anxious-preoccupied attachment drives her to over-involve herself, fearing her children will fail without her. This contrasts sharply with her children's disorganized patterns, rooted in instability. This imbalance fuels the codependent dynamic.

DSM-5 Diagnostic Overview

The clinical picture reveals a complex web of interconnected issues. Ayza's Adjustment Disorder is a direct response to the stress of managing her children's conditions, including Substance Use Disorder and ADHD, which impair their ability to function independently.

The Emotional Roller Coaster

Ayza described her emotional state as a "roller coaster." This chart visualizes her cycle of feelingsfrom the exhaustion of her daily life to determined anger, the resolve to act, and the subsequent guilt and justification that pull her back into the cycle.

The Turning Point: Action & Hope

A Plan for Empowerment

1

Take a full week off for self-care and relaxation.

2

Communicate boundaries clearly and calmly.

3

Formally remove her name from the apartment lease.

4

Force natural consequences and encourage independence.

This structured plan marks Ayza's shift from a passive `Rescuer` to an active agent in her own life. Each step is a deliberate move to dismantle the enabling system and foster genuine change.

Therapeutic Toolkit

Journaling

To capture raw emotions in the moment and prevent rationalization.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

To challenge and reframe core beliefs about guilt and responsibility.

Transactional Analysis Rescripting

To consciously rewrite her life script from 'Martyr' to 'Empowered Self'.

These evidence-based tools provide Ayza with the practical skills needed to support her emotional journey, manage guilt, and solidify her new, healthier boundaries.
